Thoughts on Progressive Enhancement

The "Understanding Progressive Enhancement" article, from A List Apart sheds light on the evolution of web development practices the transition from degradation to progressive enhancement. Throughout the mid 1990s web development primarily focused on degradation, which prioritized browsers with advanced features and often overlooked users relying on older browsers such as Netscape 4. Although this approach was functional it didn't align with Tim Berners Lees vision of creating an accessible web. In 2003 Steve Champeon and Nick Finck introduced enhancement, which brought about a paradigm shift in web development. This approach emphasizes building web experiences with a foundation, in content to ensure that all users can access the core content and functionality of websites regardless of their browser or device capabilities. By doing progressive enhancement enhances the web experience while maintaining universal accessibility and staying true to the original ethos of the World Wide Web.
